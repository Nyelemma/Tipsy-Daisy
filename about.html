From stable to statement

How a well-loved horsebox found a new calling: shaking, stirring, and making your guests go “wait—that’s a bar?!”

Meet Daisy

A warm hello from the people behind the plinth.

Tipsy Daisy isn’t a faceless company—it’s a labour of love with four wheels. We set out to build a mobile bar that feels personal: a little rustic, a little refined, and never, ever boring. The horsebox gave us a shell with soul; we filled it with brass accents, hand-picked wood, and lights soft enough to flatter every toast.

We care about the small things—the chill of a glass, the way a garnish sits, the chuckle over a custom cocktail name. That’s the boutique in us. The quirky? That’s Daisy, refusing to be just “another pop-up stand.”

The road so far

A loose timeline, because the best stories aren’t on spread-sheets.

  1. Discovery

    We find “the one” (the horsebox, that is) and get slightly over-excited in a very British way.

  2. Stripping back, dreaming big

    Blueprints, brews, and big mood-board energy. What would make someone say “I need that bar at my wedding?”

  3. Hand-built bar

    Weekends in sawdust, linseed oil, and playlists. Daisy starts to look—and feel—like herself.

  4. First pour

    Your party is the reason we do this. Every event is a new chapter, and the guest book is the sequel.

Passion, meet personality

We’re mad about well-run events—the kind where guests relax because someone competent is behind the bar. We’re equally mad about a tiny flash of playfulness, because a night out (or a Tuesday corporate do) should have a little sparkle in it.

Personal touch means we listen. Your venue quirks, your gin leanings, your “please no neon” brief—we’ll take it all and mix something that feels yours. That’s the Tipsy Daisy promise, served neat or on the rocks.
